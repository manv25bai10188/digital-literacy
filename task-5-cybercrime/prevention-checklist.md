## Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for Students

### General Digital Hygiene

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) for your University email, LinkedIn, and Instagram. Avoid SMS-based 2FA if possible to prevent SIM-swapping.
- [ ] **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank or student portal using free airport or cafe Wi-Fi. Use a trusted VPN or your mobile hotspot instead.
- [ ] **Audit App Permissions:** Go to your phone settings and revoke "Location," "Microphone," or "Contacts" access for apps that don't strictly need them.
- [ ] **Check the 'Padlock' & URL:** Before entering any credentials, ensure the site starts with `https://` and the domain name is spelled correctly (e.g., `vitbhopal.ac.in` not `vit-bhopal-login.xyz`).
- [ ] **Backup Your Data:** Keep a weekly offline backup of your coding projects and academic papers on an external hard drive to mitigate Ransomware risks.
- [ ] **Verify "Urgent" Requests:** If a friend DMs you asking for money or an OTP because of an "emergency," call them directly on a cellular line to verify it's actually them.

### Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)

- [ ] **PIN is for SENDING, not Receiving:** Remember that entering your UPI PIN is **only** required to send money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive a refund" or "collect a prize," it is a scam.
- [ ] **Verify the 'Payee' Name:** Always double-check the verified name that pops up on the UPI app (like GPay or PhonePe) before hitting 'Proceed'. Scammers often use names like "Customer Care" to look official.
