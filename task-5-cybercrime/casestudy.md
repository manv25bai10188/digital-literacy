### Case Study: The Ransomware Attack

**What the Crime Is** Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files, making them inaccessible. The attacker then demands a "ransom" payment, usually in cryptocurrency, in exchange for the decryption key. Unlike simple data theft, ransomware holds the user's digital life hostage.

**How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)**

1. **Infiltration:** A student receives an email appearing to be from the University Library or a "Free Textbook" site. They click a link to download a PDF, which actually executes a hidden script.
2. **Deployment:** The malware silently runs in the background, scanning the hard drive for high-value file types like `.docx`, `.pdf`, `.py`, and `.jpg`.
3. **Encryption:** Using a strong encryption algorithm (like AES-256), the malware locks all identified files.
4. **The Demand:** A "ReadMe" text file appears on the desktop or as a wallpaper, stating that all files are locked and providing a Bitcoin wallet address for payment.

**Who is Typically Targeted** Students and small organizations are frequent targets because they often lack robust off-site backups. Students are particularly vulnerable during exam seasons or project deadlines when losing access to their work feels catastrophic.

**The Consequences** The primary consequence is the total loss of academic work, research data, and personal photos. Even if the ransom is paid, there is no guarantee the attacker will provide the key. Furthermore, the malware often leaves "backdoors" in the system, making the device a permanent security risk.
